How to Report a Bug

In the course of using technology, bugs happen. Sometimes software does the wrong thing, or doesn't anticipate what you want to do with it.

In this new era of open-source software, it's easy to tell the people that made that software that something's wrong.

So that those wise folks can help you and fix the problem, it's important to give the right details in the right way. Here are a few hints.

Attitude

Maybe the bug ate your homework, or it's taken you hours to track down the culprit and the bug is something really silly, something that nobody would ever do.

It's okay to be annoyed, but it's not useful or productive to talk about this too much - just like adding 'ASAP' to every email, software developers are used to folks trying to set their priorities. And, like everyone else, they have a lot of priorities.

Instead, take advantage of the fact that programmers love easy problems. And if you can give enough detail for them to debug your issue quickly, it'll get fixed quickly.

Detail

Before reporting your bug, open your web browser's debug tools, look in the 'error console', and, if there's anything there, copy and paste it, in full, and add it to your bug report.
